# App Name Brainstorm

Candidates to replace "Debt Freedom". The app's real hook is the *countdown
to zero* and the one-shot kill playstyle — the best names sell that, not
"debt".

## The countdown angle (the badge IS the product)

| Name | Pitch |
|---|---|
| **Day Zero** | The day you owe nothing. Clean, dramatic, and the icon badge literally counts down to it. |
| **Freeday** | Your debt-free date, reads like "Friday." Warm: "Your Freeday is Nov 11." |
| **Owe Zero** | The goal stated in five letters. |

## The aggressive angle (save up, one-shot the loan)

| Name | Pitch |
|---|---|
| **Debtonator** | Debt + detonator. You don't pay off Lending Point, you *detonate* it. Icon: fuse/plunger. |
| **Debt Ray** | Zap accounts until they're dust. Icon writes itself. |
| **OneShot** | The literal strategy — hold until you can one-shot it and keep the emergency fund. |

## The clever/subtle angle (nothing on the home screen screams DEBT)

| Name | Pitch |
|---|---|
| **Quits** | Old English for "even, settled, owing nothing" ("we're quits"). Doubles as quitting debt. |
| **Greenline** | The rising green line in the icon. Positive, brandable, private. |
| **Freed** | Past tense, end state — and "cash flow freed" is already a concept in the app. |
| **Debtox** | Debt detox. Punchy, self-explanatory, though it still contains "debt". |

## Top three

1. **Debtonator** — most memorable, matches the kill-shot mechanics, branding is a gift.
2. **Day Zero** — most marketable straight; the whole app collapses into one promise: a date, getting closer.
3. **Quits** — most clever; the day you hit zero, the app just says "We're quits."

## Rename checklist (when one is picked)

- `index.html` title + header branding
- `manifest.json` name/short_name
- `android/app/src/main/res/values/strings.xml` app_name
- Notification strings (worker + native)
- README / release titles in the workflow
- Optional: repo + Pages URL rename (touchy — the APK points at the Pages URL)
